Setting

The world stands broken between two fallen empires. Aurelion in the north, abandoned by their sun god. The Free Kingdoms in the south, gripped by famine and drought. Between them lies the Silent Isle—and beneath it, the Barrowlands.

Unique Mechanics

Each class features distinctive mechanics that shape gameplay and character progression:

Corruption Tracks

Blighted Scholar and Cursed Fool both bear Corruption (0-10). As you gain power from forbidden sources, Corruption rises. At 10 Corruption, you lose your character to darkness.

The Pilgrimage & The Sacrifice

Sun Priest carries the Lantern of Dorvos on a sacred journey (Pilgrimage track 1-10). Lose 1 Progress per turn without the lantern. If you end a session without it, you must complete The Sacrifice before the next session ends: burn an innocent alive, immolate an ally, or sacrifice one of your own limbs—or suffer Involuntary Immolation.

Marko's Head

Prophet carries Marko's mummified head. It grants visions and prophecies, but at a cost. The more you consult it, the more it demands—and the less certain the line between its will and yours.

The Whisper

Cursed Fool bears a relic that whispers secrets and lies. It offers power, but feeds on your sanity and pushes you toward Corruption. You never know when it speaks truth.

Stone Blood

Stone Knight is immune to poison and disease, a gift from years in deep mines. You are unmovable when you choose to be—but the stone in your veins makes you slow to heal naturally.

Shadow Weaving

Murk Sage shapes darkness itself. Create nightmares, cloak allies in shadow, or step between dark places. But the more you dwell in shadow, the less you remember the light.

Delver's Instinct

Cave Wretch knows the underground like others know their own skin. Navigate by sound and scent, find hidden paths, squeeze through impossible gaps—but the surface world feels alien and hostile.
